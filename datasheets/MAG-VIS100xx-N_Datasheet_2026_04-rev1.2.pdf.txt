1. Description  
The Magics’ Vision Series portfolio contains products 
which are to be used as core building blocks of a 
radiation -hardened camera. Next to the standalone ICs 
in the Vision Series, Magics also offers full -blown 
electronic Vision modules, containing all ICs from the 
Vision Series, integrated in the form of a three -PCB 
stack up. The modules are the electronic heart of a 
radiation -hardened camera and contain at minimum 
the following Magics’ ICs from the Vision Series:  
• MAG -IMG002X1 -NC: Full HD CMOS rad -hard 
image sensor  
• MAG -PSU00001 -NP: rad -hard DC/DC 
converter controller  
• MAG -CXP00002 -NP: CoaXPress serializer IC  
Different variants with different features are offered as 
an end product (overview of product variants can be 
found in section 7), of which some also contain ICs from 
the Magics’ Motion Series. By incorporating the Motion 
Series ICs, additional functionality, such as motorized 
lens control, can be added to the module.  
Next to the Magics’ ICs all the supporting electronics, as 
described in the application diagrams in the respective 
IC datasheets, are integrated into the module. These 
components include passives, such as resistors, inductors and capacitors, as well as a XTAL oscillator for 
clock reference input. In addition, there is a connector 
to supply power (9V) and a HD -BNC connector to 
interface a 75 -Ohm coaxial cable for video transmission 
(downlink) and configuration data (up link).

The MAG -VIS1000x -N module is the version integrated into the MAG -CAM demonstrator, but it is also available as a 
radiation -hardened standalone product. While PCB1 and PCB3 are identical across the MAG -VIS1000x -N, MAG -
VIS1001x -N, and MAG -VIS1002x -N variants , the difference lies in the middle PCB (PCB2). This central board defines the 
specific functionality of each module. A block diagram of the MAG -VIS1000x -N module is shown in  Figure 5. 
At the core of the module is a Full HD image sensor, mounted on PCB1, available in both monochrome (MAG -IMG00241 -
NC) and color (MAG -IMG00251 -NC) versions. Both versions use the CLCC -104 package. The image sensor communicates 
via a CMOS parallel interface w ith the MAG -CXP00002 -NP serializer, located on PCB3. This serializer converts the parallel 
video data into a serialized stream suitable for transmission over a coaxial cable.  
All communication lines pass through PCB2, which also handles signaling between components. In addition to the 
parallel data interface, a dedicated SPI bus is used for communication between the image sensor and the serializer. This 
SPI interface is exclusi vely used for configuration and control of the image sensor by the serializer.  
The MAG -CXP00002 -NP serializer manages all communication between the image sensor and the CoaXPress (CXP) host, 
effectively presenting the module as a single logical device to the host controller. Through the 20 -MHz uplink channel 
between the frame grabber and the serializer, the host can communicate with both the serializer and the image sensor. 
The register maps are designed to appear unified, making the system fully GenICam compliant.  
In addition to the vision -related components, the MAG -VIS1000x -N module includes an extra device from Magics’ 
Motion Series: the MAG -DRV00002 -NP driver IC. This chip provides eight low -side drivers, each capable of sourcing up 
to 125 mA, ideal for applicat ions such as LED control. The driver IC is controlled via a second master SPI interface on the 
serializer, which is also accessible via the CXP uplink, allowing full remote control from the frame grabber.  
A 14 -pin connector located on the side of the module (see Figure 3 and Figure 4) provides:  
• Access to the eight low -side driver outputs.  
• A 9V power supply input.  
• Ground connections.

4.3.1 Recommended operating conditions in radiation environment  
The primary performance parameter affected by irradiation is dark current. As radiation dose accumulates, dark current 
increases, which can cause the image to saturate more quickly during long exposure times. However, in terms of 
functionality, the imager remains fully operational.  
 
Due to on -chip parameter drifts induced by radiation, some biasing parameters may require adjustment over the 
sensor's lifetime. This re -tuning is straightforward and involves uploading updated register settings via the CoaXPress 
(CXP) link —no manual inter vention on the camera hardware is necessary. Note that, regardless of irradiation, the 
camera already requires a register map upload at boot to apply the correct configuration.  
 
Additionally, dark current also increases with temperature, which is a well -known behaviour of all CMOS image sensors. 
Importantly, the sensor’s self -heating is influenced by the radiation dose rate —a higher dose rate leads to a greater 
temperature rise, f urther amplifying dark current.  
 
Recommended Operating Guidelines for High -Dose Environments (>1 kGy/hr)  
To ensure optimal sensor performance under high radiation conditions:  
• Minimize exposure time (preferably below 5 ms):  
o Longer exposures result in more integrated dark current.  
o Exposure time can be dynamically adjusted via CXP uplink commands.  
• Maintain low sensor temperature:  
o Implement active cooling solutions to prevent self -heating.  
o With effective thermal management, strict exposure time limitations may be relaxed.  
Lower Dose Rate Conditions (<0.5 kGy/hr)  
At lower dose rates, sensor self -heating is significantly reduced. As a result, dark current remains relatively stable, and 
the impact on image quality is minimal.s

More information on operating the MAG -VIS modules is available when the modules are purchased, in the form of a 
user manual. In case any additional questions arise, it is advised to contact sales@magics.tech . 
The MAG -VIS module comes with pre -defined DCF files (basically camera configuration files), which configure the 
camera for operation in different modes:  
• VGA5:  VGA resolution at 5 frames per second.  
• VGA50: VGA resolution at 50 frames per second.  
• VGA100: VGA resolution at 100 frames per second.  
• HD5: HD resolution at 5 frames per second.  
• HD40: HD resolution at 40 frames per second.  
• FHD10: FHD resolution at 10 frames per second.  
• FHD25: FHD resolution at 25 frames per second.  
• FHD30: FHD resolution at 30 frames per second.  
More information on loading the DCF files can be found in the user manual.
